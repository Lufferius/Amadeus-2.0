# 6-week curriculum

## Week 1: Basic GDS and travel concepts

Goal: Understand what Amadeus is, what a GDS is, and the difference between PNR, reservation, ticket, segment, traveller, and corporate client.

### Day 1: What is Amadeus?
Teach:
- GDS
- Amadeus
- Travel Consultant
- corporate client
- traveller
- supplier

Exercises:
- Explain what Amadeus is in simple words.
- Explain why a Travel Consultant does not simply choose the cheapest flight.

### Day 2: PNR vs reservation vs ticket
Teach:
- PNR is the reservation record.
- Ticket is the issued travel document.
- Unticketed means no ticket has been issued.
- Ticketed means a ticket exists.

Exercises:
- Explain why changing a ticketed booking is riskier than changing an unticketed booking.

### Day 3: Segments and itineraries
Teach:
- A segment is one part of a trip.
- Direct flights, connections, round trips, open jaw.

Exercises:
- Count segments in sample itineraries.

### Day 4: Required information before searching
Teach:
- origin
- destination
- dates
- times
- traveller name
- corporate policy
- baggage
- cabin
- flexibility
- approval

Exercises:
- Given incomplete travel requests, list missing information.

### Day 5: Week 1 review
Quiz:
- GDS
- Amadeus
- PNR
- ticket
- unticketed
- segment
- corporate policy
- data needed before searching

Pass criteria:
- The learner can clearly explain PNR vs ticket.

- contact details
- ticketing time limit
- remarks

- OSI
- seats

- frequent flyer
- company references

Exercises:

### Day 2: Passenger name and critical data

- compound names
- spelling risk
- do not use nicknames

Exercises:
- Choose correct passenger names from examples.

### Day 3: Contact details and remarks
Teach:
- documenting requests
- documenting approvals
- documenting fare decisions
- internal notes

Exercises:

### Day 4: Ticketing time limit

- fares may not be guaranteed before ticketing

Exercises:

## Week 3: SSR, OSI, seats, baggage, special services

Goal: Understand special requests and avoid promising unconfirmed services.

### Day 1: SSR
Teach:
- Special Service Request
- can require airline confirmation
- examples: special meal, wheelchair, medical service, assistance

Exercises:
- Classify requests as SSR or not.

### Day 2: OSI
Teach:
- Other Service Information
- informational
- does not replace SSR

Exercises:
- Classify items as SSR or OSI.

### Day 3: Seats
Teach:
- aisle, window, emergency exit
- availability
- fare restrictions
- paid seats
- confirmation risk

Exercises:
- Decide what can and cannot be promised.

### Day 4: Baggage
Teach:
- baggage may or may not be included
- compare total cost
- corporate policy
- paid extras

Exercises:
- Compare fare options with and without baggage.

### Day 5: Week 3 review
Quiz:
- SSR
- OSI
- seats
- baggage
- special service confirmation

## Week 5: Changes, refunds, voids, reissues, queues

### Day 1: Changes before and after ticketing
Teach:
- unticketed changes
- ticketed changes
- repricing
- exchange/reissue risk

Exercises:
- Classify cases by risk level.

### Day 2: Reissue / exchange
Teach:
- changing an issued ticket
- penalty
- fare difference
- used/unused ticket
- internal procedure

Exercises:
- List what must be checked before changing an issued return flight.

### Day 3: Refund, void, cancellation
Teach:
- cancel segment
- void
- refund
- no-show
- refund is not automatic

Exercises:
- True/false questions.

### Day 4: Schedule changes and involuntary changes
Teach:
- airline-driven change
- voluntary vs involuntary
- possible waivers
- connection impact

Exercises:
- Classify changes as voluntary or involuntary.

### Day 5: Queues

- queues are work baskets
- schedule changes
- deadlines
- airline messages
- pending confirmations

Exercises:
- Explain what to do when a schedule change appears.

### Day 1: Missing information microcases
Exercises:

### Day 2: Fare microcases

### Day 3: SSR/OSI microcases
Exercises:
- Classify requests and identify confirmation risks.

### Day 4: Change microcases
Exercises:
- Compare unticketed vs ticketed changes.

### Day 5: Final assessment
Assessment:
- theory test
- guided corporate travel case
- identify missing data
- identify fare risks
- identify SSR/OSI
- explain what not to promise
- explain what to escalate
